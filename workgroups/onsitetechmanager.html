On-site Tech Manager

Role description

Before

  • Do an on-site venue check to ensure that each track has got all the necessary equipment prepared.
  • Check if the wifi on-site is sufficient for all the attendees and if not, source for possible providers
  • Make a list of all the equipment required for each room.
  • Prepare a box of all materials required in each room in advance and label them. Hand them out to session chair of each track.
  • Keep in contact with the on-site technicians and save their numbers on the phone.
  • Prepare 3 spare laptops for 3 tracks to run in between videos and timing at a corner to indicate break time.
  • Make sure all required A/V resources are available and working (sound, etc)
  • Check/set display resolution

During

  • prepares sound check schedule for all speakers in their assigned track and liaise with session chairs to ensure that every speaker has gone through their sound checks
  • Keep in contact with all session chair to ensure smooth operational flow during talks.

After

  • Gather all materials from each room and safe keep for next year

Guidelines

Get projector and run slide show at registration (announcements, lost/found items, open space, etc.)

  • Spare adapters per room
  • Perhaps a spare projector in the speaker's ready room which can be used to test the speaker's equipment out prior to their talk
  • A PyCon logo (w/year) to cover up the hotel logo placard on the podiums so that we don't have the hotel name in all the videos AND we have a reminder of the year -- perhaps this idea could mutate into having a sign with the speaker's name and talk title for visibility on the video.

Venue check

  • Does the venue have microphones?
  • Does the venue have video projectors? The size large enough for attendees?

Keeping track

  • Headset mics to free up both hands?
  • Buffer zone around A/V setup to avoid RFI
Also check on step 12 of quick guide for reference.
