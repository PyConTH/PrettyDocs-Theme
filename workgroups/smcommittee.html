Social Media Committee

Role description

Social Media Committe

Throughout entire event:

  • Manages contents in Twitter, Facebook and Instagram
  • Prepares a timeline of contents and posts for social media
  • Liaise with Program WG to gather the speakers’ titles so as to plan in advance
  • Plan promotion for certain giveaways.
  • Promote sales of tickets, venue confirmation
  • Work with the program lead to promote contents of the talks from speakers.
  • Liaise with Design committee to get assets for social media contents

Before event:

  • Ensure marketing contents are scheduled and spread evenly
  • Create timeline to spread contents evenly in advance. Consider using buffer.
  • Create an event on Facebook

During

  • allocate team members to post live videos, contents, tweets about events. Constant reminder on using official hashtag for event.

After

  • Follow-up with team on their work and evaluate on what needs to be improved for next year’s content.
Guidelines
Contents example:
  • CFP opening
  • CFP closing
  • Tickets up for sale
  • Early bird ticket on sale
  • Confirmed keynote speakers
  • Sponsors mentions
  • Talk contents
  • party contents
  • workshop contents
  • sprint contents
  • official swags
  • official hashtags

Facebook

  • Buffer will not be usable for facebook if posts is intended to be bilingual, and tagging the event onto post. (Buffer does not supports that.)
  • Always remember to include official hashtag
  • tag related sponsors or speakers
  • encourage organising team to share the posts.
  • share the posts in other python groups

Instagram

  • Use more attractive photos
  • lesser words
  • Always remember to include official hashtag
  • tag related sponsors or speakers

Twitter

  • Use catchy phrase
  • many speakers use twitter. When posting their speakers’ talks or sponsors mentions, always remember to include their handles and the official hashtag.
  • tweets doesn’t need to include photos everytime.

Contents guidelines

  • linking to press contents that covers more of a specific company than the event
  • always wait for official announcements before posting out on official channels
  • Check the code of conduct on the contents to ensure that posts comply to the codes.
  • keep the event theme in mind when creating contents
  • Keep writing tones personal not private
  • When in doubt, don’t post
  • keep sentences simple and short to ensure that the content fits most audience.
  • choose powerful photos to go with contents
  • always remember to include event hashtags.
